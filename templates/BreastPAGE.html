Input Data Explanation :

Diagnosis: The diagnosis of breast tissue (M = malignant, B = benign).

Malignant (M): refers to cancerous tumors. Malignant tumors are those composed of abnormal cells that can invade and destroy nearby tissues and can spread (metastasize) to other parts of the body. Malignant tumors are typically considered harmful and may require aggressive treatment such as surgery, chemotherapy, or radiation therapy.

Benign (B): refers to non-cancerous tumors. Benign tumors are composed of normal cells that do not invade nearby tissues or spread to other parts of the body. While benign tumors are not typically life-threatening, they may still cause health issues depending on their size and location. In many cases, benign tumors may be monitored by healthcare professionals but may not require immediate treatment.

Radius Mean: Mean of distances from center to points on the perimeter.

Texture Mean: Standard deviation of gray-scale values.

Perimeter Mean: Mean size of the core tumor.

Area Mean: Mean area of the core tumor.

Smoothness Mean: Mean of local variation in radius lengths.

Compactness Mean: Measure of compactness (perimeter^2 / area - 1.0).

Concavity Mean: Severity of concave portions of the contour.

Concave Points Mean: Number of concave portions of the contour.

Symmetry Mean: Symmetry of cell nuclei.

Fractal Dimension Mean: "Coastline approximation" - 1.

Radius SE: Standard error of the mean of distances from center to points on the perimeter.

Texture SE: Standard error of gray-scale values.

Perimeter SE: Standard error of the size of the core tumor.

Area SE: Standard error of the area of the core tumor.

Smoothness SE: Standard error of local variation in radius lengths.

Compactness SE: Standard error of the measure of compactness.

Concavity SE: Standard error of the severity of concave portions of the contour.

Concave Points SE: Standard error for the number of concave portions of the contour.

Symmetry SE: Standard error of symmetry of cell nuclei.

Fractal Dimension SE: Standard error for "coastline approximation".
