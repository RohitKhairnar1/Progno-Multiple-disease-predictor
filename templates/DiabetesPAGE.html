Input Data Explanation :

Pregnancies : The number of times a participant has been pregnant. This is a numerical value ranging from 0 to 17, indicating the number of pregnancies a woman has had.

Glucose : Plasma glucose concentration a 2 hours in an oral glucose tolerance test. This is a measure of blood sugar levels and is crucial for diabetes diagnosis. The range is from 0 to 199 mg/dL.

BloodPressure : Diastolic blood pressure, which is the pressure in the lower part of the heart when it pumps blood to the rest of the body. The range is from 0 to 122 mm Hg.

SkinThickness : Triceps skinfold thickness measured in millimeters. This is an indicator of the amount of subcutaneous fat. The range is from 0 to 99 mm.

Insulin : 2-Hour serum insulin measured in microunits per milliliter (mu U/ml). This is a measure of insulin levels in the blood, which is important for diabetes management and diagnosis. The range is from 0 to 846 mu U/ml.

BMI : Body Mass Index, calculated as weight in kilograms divided by the square of height in meters. This is a measure of body fat and is used to assess a person's risk of developing health problems like diabetes. The range is from 0 to 67.1 kg/m^2.

Diabetes Pedigree Function : This is a measure of the likelihood of developing diabetes based on family history. It ranges from 0.078 to 2.42, with higher values indicating a higher risk.

Age : The age of the participants, ranging from 21 to 81 years. This is a crucial factor in diabetes risk assessment as age is a significant risk factor for diabetes.
