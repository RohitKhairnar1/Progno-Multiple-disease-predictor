Explanation Description
Age The age of the patient in years.
Blood Pressure The systolic over diastolic reading of the patient's blood pressure.
Specific Gravity A measure of the concentration of solutes in urine compared to water.
Albumin A protein found in the blood; its presence in urine may indicate kidney damage.
Sugar The level of sugar in the urine, which could indicate diabetes or other metabolic disorders.
Red Blood Cells The presence and condition of red blood cells in urine.
Pus Cell The presence and condition of pus cells in urine, possibly indicating a urinary tract infection.
Pus Cell Clumps The presence of clumps of pus cells in urine, further indicating a urinary tract infection.
Bacteria The presence of bacteria in urine, indicating a potential urinary tract infection.
Blood Glucose Random The random blood glucose level, indicating the concentration of glucose in the blood at a random time.
Blood Urea The blood urea level, indicating the concentration of urea nitrogen in the blood, a waste product of protein metabolism.
Serum Creatinine The serum creatinine level, indicating kidney function. Creatinine is a waste product of muscle metabolism.
Sodium The sodium level in the blood, an electrolyte essential for various bodily functions.
Potassium The potassium level in the blood, an electrolyte crucial for nerve and muscle function.
Hemoglobin The hemoglobin level in the blood, which carries oxygen to tissues and removes carbon dioxide.
Packed Cell Volume The packed cell volume (hematocrit), a measure of the volume percentage of red blood cells in the blood.
White Blood Cell Count The number of white blood cells per volume of blood, indicating the immune system's response to infection or inflammation.
Red Blood Cell Count The number of red blood cells per volume of blood, indicating oxygen-carrying capacity.
Hypertension The presence of hypertension (high blood pressure).
Diabetes Mellitus The presence of diabetes mellitus, a metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels.
Coronary Artery Disease The presence of coronary artery disease, a condition where the blood vessels supplying the heart muscle become narrowed or blocked.
Appetite The patient's appetite status.
Pedal Edema The presence of pedal edema, swelling of the feet and ankles due to fluid retention.
Anemia The presence of anemia, a condition where there is a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin in the blood.
