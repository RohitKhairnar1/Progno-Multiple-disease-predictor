Input Data Explanation :

Age: Age of the patient in years.

Blood Pressure: The blood pressure level of the patient.

Specific Gravity: Specific gravity is a measure of the concentration of solutes in urine compared with water. It indicates the kidney's ability to concentrate urine.

Albumin: Albumin is a protein found in the blood. Its presence in urine can indicate kidney damage.

Sugar: Sugar level in the urine. Elevated sugar levels can indicate diabetes or other metabolic disorders.

Red Blood Cells: Presence and condition of red blood cells in urine.

Pus Cell: Presence and condition of pus cells in urine.

Pus Cell Clumps: Presence of clumps of pus cells in urine, indicating possible urinary tract infection.

Bacteria: Presence of bacteria in urine, indicating possible urinary tract infection.

Blood Glucose Random: Random blood glucose level, indicating the concentration of glucose in the blood at a random time.

Blood Urea: Blood urea level, indicating the concentration of urea nitrogen in the blood, a waste product of protein metabolism.

Serum Creatinine: Serum creatinine level, indicating kidney function. Creatinine is a waste product of muscle metabolism.

Sodium: Sodium level in the blood, an electrolyte essential for various bodily functions.

Potassium: Potassium level in the blood, an electrolyte crucial for nerve and muscle function.

Hemoglobin: Hemoglobin level in the blood, which carries oxygen to tissues and removes carbon dioxide.

Packed Cell Volume: Packed cell volume (hematocrit), a measure of the volume percentage of red blood cells in the blood.

White Blood Cell Count: Number of white blood cells per volume of blood, indicating the immune system's response to infection or inflammation.

Red Blood Cell Count: Number of red blood cells per volume of blood, indicating oxygen-carrying capacity.

Hypertension: Presence of hypertension (high blood pressure).

Diabetes Mellitus: Presence of diabetes mellitus, a metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels.

Coronary Artery Disease: Presence of coronary artery disease, a condition where the blood vessels supplying the heart muscle become narrowed or blocked.

Appetite: Patient's appetite status.

Pedal Edema: Presence of pedal edema, swelling of the feet and ankles due to fluid retention.

Anemia: Presence of anemia, a condition where there is a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin in the blood.
